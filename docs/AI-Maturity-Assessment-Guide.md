# AI-Augmented SDLC: Maturity Assessment Guide

## Introduction
The AI Maturity Assessment is a structured framework designed to evaluate how deeply and effectively AI is integrated into our delivery lifecycle. Rather than a "test" of your skills, this is a collaborative exercise to identify **high-value patterns**, bridge **adoption gaps**, and move our teams from ad-hoc experimentation toward a **governed, high-multiplier state.**

Our goal is to reach the **AI Multiplier (V×E×Q)**: significantly increasing Velocity, Efficiency, and Quality through intentional AI augmentation.

---

## 1. The 5 Levels of AI Maturity

We categorize team maturity into five distinct levels:

| Level | Name | Description |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **L1** | **Ad-Hoc** | Individual, uncoordinated AI use. No shared prompts or official tools. AI use is often a "dark secret." |
| **L2** | **Enabled** | Team has basic tool access (e.g., Codex, Copilot). AI is used for boilerplate and unit tests, but with high variability. |
| **L3** | **Integrated** | AI is part of the "Definition of Ready/Done." The team uses a shared prompt library and consistent workflows. |
| **L4** | **Optimized** | Measurable impact on Velocity/Quality. The team uses automated agents and proactively automates bottlenecks. |
| **L5** | **Augmented** | AI-First mindset. Governance is self-improving. Human-AI agency is seamless; humans focus only on high-order logic. |

---

## 2. What We Assess (The 4 Personas)

The assessment covers the entire Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) across four key areas:

### 💡 Product (Discovery & Strategy)
*   **Requirements:** Using AI to expand rough notes into structured user stories.
*   **Analysis:** Systematically identifying edge cases and Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs).
*   **Prioritization:** Using AI-assisted frameworks (like RICE) to rank backlogs.

### 🛠️ Engineering (Development)
*   **Scaffolding:** Using AI for boilerplate, DTOs, Mappers, and API controllers.
*   **Refactoring:** Identifying code smells and legacy modernization patterns.
*   **Code Review:** Using AI for an initial "first pass" on PRs for security and style.

### 🧪 QA (Validation & Quality)
*   **Scenario Design:** Generating positive, negative, and boundary tests from requirements.
*   **Automation:** Accelerating the creation of Playwright, Cypress, or Selenium scripts.
*   **Data/Defects:** Generating synthetic test data and AI-augmented bug reports.

### 🛡️ Risks & Governance
*   **Security:** Ensuring PII/sensitive data is never shared with external LLMs.
*   **Sovereignty:** Maintaining the "Human-in-the-Loop" (Verify, Don't Just Trust).

---

## 3. The "Evidence-Based" Interview

During the assessment, we use the **"Show Me" Protocol.** For any high score (Level 4 or 5), we will ask to see:
1.  **Shared Prompts:** The actual text or templates the team uses.
2.  **Live Demos:** A quick look at how you use your IDE (e.g., Cursor, Codex) or Figma-to-code pipelines.
3.  **Context Files:** Any documentation (like architectural `.md` files) used to guide AI agents.

*Note: If you've encountered AI "hallucinations" or errors, please share them! Understanding how you caught and fixed an error is a sign of high maturity.*

---

## 4. Outcomes
After the interview, your team will receive:
*   **A Maturity Scorecard:** Showing where you stand in each of the four areas.
*   **Strengths Report:** Recognition of the high-value patterns your team has invented.
*   **A "Path to Next Level":** Three specific technical or process goals (a, b, and c) to help your team reach the next maturity tier.
*   **Prioritized Recommendations:** A roadmap of 10 actionable steps to increase your AI Multiplier.
